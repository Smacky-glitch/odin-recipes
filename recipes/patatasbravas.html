Authentic Patatas Bravas

Description

Authentic Patatas Bravas are a sacred Spanish institution and absolutely not just fried potatoes with sauce. No, these are different potatoes — cut with passion, fried with destiny, and topped with a sauce recipe that is apparently guarded more closely than national secrets. As a gringo, I approach the plate with deep respect, nodding thoughtfully while thinking, “Yes, these are… definitely potatoes,” and pretending I can detect the subtle regional nuances between spicy tomato sauce and slightly spicier tomato sauce.

Eating patatas bravas also comes with an unspoken performance requirement: you must confidently declare them “the best you’ve ever had” while gesturing vaguely at the plate, even if you burned your mouth and can no longer taste anything. I will insist they are superior to any version I’ve had back home, mostly because saying otherwise feels dangerous. In the end, I leave full, happy, and mildly confused, wondering how something so simple can inspire such passion — and secretly hoping no one asks me to explain what makes them authentic.

Ingredients

  • 2 Russet Potatos peeled and cut into 1-inch cuber
  • 2 cups of Olive Oil
  • 1 tablespoon of Salt
  • 3 tablespoons of Olive Oil
  • 1 Onion diced
  • 1 teaspoon Salt
  • 1 Garlic Clove finely chopped
  • 1 Red Chile minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon of Smoked Prprika
  • 14 oz of Whole Peeled Tomatos
  • 1/4 cup of Mayonnaise

Steps

  1. STEP 1 Combine potatoes, 2 cups olive oil, and 3 teaspoons salt in a large cold skillet. Heat on low and cook until potatoes are softened, 12 to 15 minutes. Increase heat to high and fry until golden, 5 to 6 minutes. Drain on paper towels.
  2. STEP 2 Heat 3 tablespoons olive oil in a large saucepan over medium heat. Cook and stir onion with 1 teaspoon salt in the hot oil until onion has softened, 3 to 4 minutes. Add garlic, chile, and smoked paprika; simmer for 1 to 2 minutes. Stir in tomatoes and return to a simmer. Transfer tomato mixture to a blender, cover, and puree until tomato sauce is smooth.
  3. STEP 3 Serve patatas bravas with tomato puree and and mayonnaise for dipping.
  4. STEP 4 Enjoy!
