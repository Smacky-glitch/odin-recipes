Description

Irish Potatos are a traditional Philadelphia confection made from a smooth mixture of cream cheese, butter, powdered sugar, and coconut, shaped into small balls and rolled in cinnamon to resemble tiny potatos. Dispite the name, it contains no potatos at all. The "potato" look comes purely from its size, shape, and cinnamon coating. The dessert is especially popular around St. Patrick's Day and is closely associated with Irish-American communities in the region.

The treat is belived to have originated in the late 19th or early 20th century, likely as a homemade candy among working-class families. Over time, it became a local speciality, now produced by well-known Philadelphia bakeries. While it remains relatively unknown outside the area, Irish Potatos are a beloved part of Philadelphia's food culture and a nostalgic seasonal favorite. Enjoy!

Ingredients

  • 4oz of softened Cream Cheese
  • 1/4 cup of softened Butter
  • 4 cups of Confectioners' Sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of Vinilla Extract
  • 2 and 1/2 cups Flaked Coconut
  • 1 tablespoon of Ground Cinnamon

Steps

  1. STEP 1 Beat cream cheese and butter together in a large bowl until smooth. Add confectioners' sugar and vanilla; beat until smooth. Mix in flaked coconut.
  2. STEP 2 Roll mixture into balls or potato shapes; roll balls in cinnamon. Place onto a cookie sheet and chill to set for at least 10 minutes. If desired, roll potatos in cinnamon again for a darker color.
  3. STEP 3 Enjoy!
